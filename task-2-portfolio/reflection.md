What Each Platform Is Used For
GitHub is a platform for hosting and sharing code. Developers use it to showcase projects, contribute to open source, and collaborate with others. As a CSE student, this will be my primary portfolio platform.

LinkedIn is a professional networking platform used by employers, recruiters, and students. It works like a digital resume and helps in finding internships, jobs, and industry connections.

ResearchGate is a premier professional social networking site for scientists and researchers to connect, collaborate, and share their work, boasting over 25 million members globally. Founded in 2008, it acts as an open-access repository for academic publications, allowing users to find research, share papers, and ask.

How I Plan to Use Them Over the Next Four Years
GitHub – upload all projects, assignments, and open source contributions semester by semester
LinkedIn – build connections, apply for internships from the 2nd year onwards, get recommendations
ResearchGate – a professional social networking site for scientists and researchers to share publications, access open-access research, and collaborate
These three platforms together form the foundation of My professional digital identity as a CSE student.
